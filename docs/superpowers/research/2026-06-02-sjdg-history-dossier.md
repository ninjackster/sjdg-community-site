## B. Notable people & families
- **Founders:** four brothers **Hernández (Padilla)** — Francisco, Salvador, Antonio Rafael, José Antonio — settled "El Bramido" turn of the 19th c. (year **1793** widely cited but in tension with "took shape early 19th c." → Low–Med; *no founding document survives*).
- **First parish priest:** Pbro. **Fermín Padilla** (parish erected 15 May 1910) — Med.
- **Cristero figures (regional, Tepatitlán/Altos — none confirmed born in SJdG):** Pbro. Gral. **José Reyes Vega** (killed at the Battle of Tepatitlán, 19 Apr 1929); **Victoriano Ramírez "El Catorce"** (killed by his own side, 16 Mar 1929, Tepatitlán palace); Pbro. **Tranquilino Ubiarco** (hanged 5 Oct 1928, canonized 2000); **Anacleto González Flores** (Tepatitlán-born ideologue, d. 1 Apr 1927); Mayor **Quirino Navarro** (federal-side defender). — High.
- **Los Altos ranchero surnames (regional context):** ~117 founding Spanish families (criollo Castilian/Basque/Andalusian/Galician); recurring **González, Hernández, Padilla** (Padilla-Dávila among founders). — Med.

## C. US-migration story (Los Altos → US)
- Late-19th-c. origin, catalyzed by **Porfiriato railroads**; intensified by Revolution + Cristero displacement (1910s–30s); **Bracero era 1942–64** cemented networks. — High.
- Why heavy sending region: dispersed dairy/ranchero economy on small private *ranchos*, small-enterprise tradition, large households → chain migration. — High.
- Destinations (Jaliscienses in US, 2018): CA ~58%, CO ~10%, AZ ~7%, TX ~6% — Med. Jalisco = Mexico's #2 remittance state (US$2,796M, 2017); Tepatitlán among most remittance-benefited — Med–High.
- **Clubes de oriundos (HTAs)** dense in US (esp. LA); no SJdG-specific club confirmed → research lead. — High (regional).
- Spiritual: **Santo Toribio Romo** (Jalostotitlán, Los Altos; canonized 2000) — patron of migrants/"mojados." — High.

## E. Cristero War + parish detail (primary: Tepatitlán Archivo Histórico, Boletín No. 14 — High)
- **26 Oct 1926** Cañón de Troneras ambush · **8 Dec 1926** Milpillas taken · **First siege 13–15 Jan 1927** (Mayor Quirino Navarro repels ~700 Cristeros) · **Apr 1927** forced *reconcentración* (5k→20k) + Second siege (San Antonio tower; 3 Cristeros burned) · **6 Jan 1928** third reconcentración + smallpox/measles epidemic + famine/pillage · **5 Oct 1928** Ubiarco hanged · **16 Mar 1929** Cristeros retake Tepatitlán; "El Catorce" killed by own side · **★ 18–19 Apr 1929 Battle of Tepatitlán** — ~800 Cristeros under Pbro. Gral. José Reyes Vega ambush 500 federals + 1,500 agraristas (from San Luis Potosí); federal dead 120 official / 600–800 by eyewitnesses (Meyer ~225); only 28 Cristeros dead incl. Vega (shot in the forehead); rolled-up left sleeve to tell friend from agrarista — landmark Cristero victory · **21 Jun 1929** *arreglos*; **23 Jun 1929** Tepatitlán Cristeros surrender arms at Cine Morelos.
